Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 16, 2026

Short version: VisStack does not collect, transmit, or store any of your data. All audio processing happens locally in your browser and never leaves your device.

1. What VisStack does

VisStack is a Chrome extension that overlays real-time, audio-reactive visualizations on top of the web page you are currently listening to. When you activate it, the extension asks for your permission to capture audio from the current tab (via Chrome's standard tab-sharing dialog) so it can analyze the audio frequencies, beats, and energy levels to drive the visualization.

2. What we collect

Nothing. VisStack does not collect, log, or transmit any personal information, browsing history, audio content, URLs, IP addresses, device identifiers, or any other data about you or your activity. There are no analytics, no telemetry, no crash reporting, and no third-party trackers embedded in the extension.

3. What we store locally

The extension saves your visualization preferences to your browser's localStorage so they persist between sessions. These preferences never leave your device and are not synced, uploaded, or shared with anyone. The only keys VisStack writes are:

  • viz-ext-state-v1 — the id of the last visualization you had active in the extension
  • viz-state-v1 — (in the companion web app only) the active visualization id, per-plugin slider values, and your recently used audio URLs

You can clear these at any time by removing the extension or clearing your browser's site data.

4. Audio capture

To analyze audio, VisStack uses your browser's built-in getDisplayMedia API. Chrome shows its own native tab picker so you can explicitly choose which tab to share, and Chrome displays a persistent "Sharing this tab" indicator while capture is active. VisStack never receives audio you haven't explicitly consented to share. The captured audio is processed in memory for real-time analysis only — it is never recorded, saved, or transmitted anywhere.

5. Permissions

VisStack requests the following permissions for the reasons described:

  • activeTab — lets the extension interact with the tab you explicitly activate by clicking its toolbar icon.
  • <all_urls> (host permission) — lets the extension run on any website where you activate it, so it can visualize audio from any source. No page content is read; VisStack only acts on the page after you click its icon.

6. Third-party services

VisStack does not integrate with or send data to any third-party services. The extension bundles the open-source libraries listed on its GitHub repository (butterchurn, three.js, React) which run entirely within your browser; none of them phone home.

7. Children's privacy

VisStack does not knowingly collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.

8. Changes to this policy

If this policy changes materially in the future, the "Last updated" date at the top of this page will be revised and the Chrome Web Store listing will link to the new version. Continued use of VisStack after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

9. Contact

Questions or concerns about this policy can be raised as a GitHub issue on the VisStack repository.
